# Attestation Guide

## What Are Attestations?

Attestations are verifiable claims about your work and skills, signed by teammates who witnessed your work. They live on LinkedTrust as proof of what you've accomplished.

## Types of Attestations

### 1. Completion Attestation
**Claim**: "Completed LinkedTrust Onboarding Program"
**Who Signs**: Team lead + peer reviewers
**When**: After all core tasks complete

### 2. Skill Attestations
**Claims**: Specific skills demonstrated
- "Proficient with LinkedTrust SDK"
- "Creates clear technical documentation"
- "Gives constructive peer reviews"
**Who Signs**: Teammates who directly observed the skill
**When**: As you demonstrate skills

### 3. Project Contributions
**Claims**: Work delivered on specific projects
- "Contributed to [project name]"
- "Improved onboarding documentation"
**Who Signs**: Project lead or teammates
**When**: After deliverable is complete and merged

## How to Request Attestations

1. Complete your work
2. Get peer reviews
3. Create issue using the Request Attestation template (coming soon)
4. Link evidence (closed issues, merged PRs, deliverables)
5. Tag reviewers who can vouch for the work
6. Lead reviews and creates attestation

## Tips

- **Be specific**: "Used LinkedTrust SDK to create claims" > "Did coding"
- **Link evidence**: Make it easy to verify
- **Get peer reviews first**: Attestations should confirm what peers already verified
- **Quality over quantity**: One strong attestation > many weak ones

## Certificate Publishing

After receiving attestations, you can publish them as certificates on linkedtrust.us. Use these for:
- Portfolio/resume
- Showing skills to future clients/employers
- Building reputation in the community
